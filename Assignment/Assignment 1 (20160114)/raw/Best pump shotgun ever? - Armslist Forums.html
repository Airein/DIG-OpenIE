              I personally like the Mossbergs that don't have a lifter mechanism in the way of the loading gate. Under duress it is very easy to get hung up on it and/or cut your fingers, etc. 3 gunners and such grind and polish the lifter on their guns and you can do that too on a defensive 870, but I say why even bother? If it's not there, it's not a problem. The 590 is more heavy duty than the standard 500 model as well. Plus as a lefty I like the tang safety vs the crossbolt safety on the Remingtons and others.

              I would say the Mossberg lifter system makes takedown and reassembly a little bit harder as opposed to an 870, but once you've done it a few times it's not hard at all.

              Some people swear by Ithaca's or Winchester 1200s as well for defensive use.

                            This is my first post to the group and I would like to thank you all for having me here.... Well, as far as shotguns go I have everything from a old Mossberg 500 defender I paid 50 bucks for. I have a SPAS12 which is as impractical as a firearm can get and everything in-between . It's eye candy. Nothing I would risk my or my family lives upon. I also own a myriad of tactical auto-loaders from a pair of decked out Benelli Super m90 M1's, Remington 1100 tactical and to the pumps, Winchester 12's, Browining pumps. And the life savers are varied built Remington 870's I have on in orange finish with LTL rounds in it as well as two 870 Police Magnums with SureFire forends and worked over by Wilson Combat and ScatterGun Tech as one is the Border Patrol models and it's the one I rest my life on. I carried it in my cruiser as it was all decked out with the light and a folding stock (Side) I forget who makes the stock it but it's great nor cheap... In my opinion the 870 or the Ithica Police models are total work-horses. I have had autoloders go DOWN in training at work- that does include the M1 and M4 Benelli but it was an ammo issue but a pump gun wont care as long as it's clean and maintained and I rest my laurels on the 870 platform. There are tons of accessory add-ons some are awesome yet some dumb as a bag of hammers. I used a scattergun at work as a street cop and the 870 NEVER failed me nor did the Ithica's we had when I first started and that's what the dept. had before going 870 then M1s90 and AR carbines at the same time through a GSA buy for the dept. I was the armorer and made the call. All 870's for the patrol. Tac SBR Mossberg AOW for undercover SHTF arm. Never put the 500/590 to the test but I did own a 590 for a while and it was a well built, easy to field clean reliable gun. Bottom line is what you like, your budget and brand loyalty. 870 gets my vote but the others can hang just as well. You get what you pay for and ammo is truly 90% of the equation. I run slug/buck back to back in all mine. and same on the saddle for reloading. I have not had any time on the Benelli pumps so I can't speak and I'm waiting on my Kel-tec 14rd to show but I would not put my life on it without empirical evidence of study before comment and getting behind it. (which I can't wait to do!!) I just always knew when I had to get the shotgun out of the lock in the cruiser I was grabbing something I did not have to second guess.... I can't say more for the 870s
